<objective>
## Goal
AI fills all castle slots before moving the leader, returns leader to keep when gold is available, and recruits a mix of unit types.

## Purpose
The current AI wastes gold because: (1) the leader moves off keep before all castle slots are filled, preventing further recruitment on subsequent turns, and (2) it always picks the most expensive unit, leading to homogeneous armies. This makes the AI feel incompetent.

## Output
Modified `norrust_core/src/ai.rs` and `norrust_core/src/ffi.rs` with smarter recruitment behavior.
</objective>

<context>
## Source Files
@norrust_core/src/ai.rs (full AI logic — ai_take_turn, ai_plan_turn, score_attack)
@norrust_core/src/ffi.rs lines 576-642 (norrust_ai_recruit — FFI recruitment)

## Key Facts
- `norrust_ai_recruit` is called BEFORE `ai_plan_turn` in the Lua love.update loop
- Recruitment requires leader on a "keep" tile with adjacent "castle" tiles empty
- If leader moved off keep last turn, next turn's recruit call finds nothing and recruits 0
- `ai_take_turn`/`ai_plan_turn` process all units without distinguishing the leader
- GameState has: positions (HashMap<u32, Hex>), units (HashMap<u32, Unit>), board, gold[2]
- Unit abilities include "leader" string for leader identification
- Board tiles have terrain_id: "keep", "castle", etc.
</context>

<acceptance_criteria>

## AC-1: Leader stays on keep until castle slots are full
```gherkin
Given an AI faction with a leader on a keep hex, gold available, and empty adjacent castle hexes
When the AI plans its turn
Then the leader does NOT move until all affordable castle slots are filled
```

## AC-2: Leader returns to keep when gold is available
```gherkin
Given an AI faction with a leader NOT on keep, gold >= cheapest recruit cost, and empty castle hexes adjacent to keep
When the AI plans its turn
Then the leader moves toward the keep hex (if reachable) instead of toward enemies
```

## AC-3: Mixed unit type recruitment
```gherkin
Given an AI faction with multiple recruitable unit types and sufficient gold
When the AI recruits units
Then it selects a variety of unit types (not always the most expensive)
```

  <name>Task 1: Leader-aware turn planning in ai.rs</name>
  <files>norrust_core/src/ai.rs</files>
  <action>
    Modify `ai_take_turn` and `ai_plan_turn` to handle the leader specially:

    1. Add a helper `fn find_leader(state: &GameState, faction: u8) -> Option<u32>` that returns the leader's unit ID.

    2. Add a helper `fn should_leader_stay(state: &GameState, faction: u8) -> bool` that returns true when:
       - Leader is on a keep tile
       - Faction has gold >= cheapest recruit cost (from unit list)
       - At least one adjacent castle hex is empty
       This requires passing available recruit costs. Add a parameter or compute from state.

    3. Add a helper `fn keep_hex_for_faction(state: &GameState, faction: u8) -> Option<Hex>` that finds the keep hex associated with this faction (look for keep tiles; if leader is on one, use that; otherwise find the keep closest to faction's side of the board).

    4. In the unit processing loop of both `ai_take_turn` and `ai_plan_turn`:
       - If the current unit is the leader AND `should_leader_stay` returns true: skip the leader entirely (don't move it)
       - If the current unit is the leader AND leader is NOT on keep AND faction has gold >= cheapest recruit AND there are empty castle hexes adjacent to keep: move leader toward keep instead of toward enemies
       - Otherwise: process normally

    5. To support `should_leader_stay`, add a `cheapest_recruit_cost` field to a new `AiContext` struct that's passed into the planning functions, OR add it as a parameter. The simplest approach: add `recruit_costs: &[u32]` parameter to `ai_take_turn` and `ai_plan_turn`.

    Note: `ai_plan_turn` must also be updated since it duplicates the logic. Consider extracting shared logic into a helper that both functions call — e.g., `fn plan_unit_action(state: &GameState, uid: u32, faction: u8, enemies: &[(u32, Hex, Unit)], recruit_costs: &[u32]) -> Option<(Hex, Option<u32>)>` returning (destination, optional attack target).

    1. Replace the `max_by_key(cost)` selection with a round-robin or weighted random approach:

       - Prefer unit types not yet recruited

    3. Also pass the recruit cost list to `ai_plan_turn` / `ai_take_turn` by collecting costs from the registry before calling the AI functions. Add the `recruit_costs` parameter to the FFI wrapper functions `norrust_ai_plan_turn` and `norrust_ai_take_turn`.
